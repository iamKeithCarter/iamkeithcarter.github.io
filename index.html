code is...

RELAXING: As I sat engaged in our cohort, I remembered my first coding language (Fortran) and wondered what had been so difficult about coding before. Putting lines of code on the screen, formatting them, building, fingers typing rhythmically was not only productive but soothing, satisfying. As line after line of code flowed from my hands my mind seemed to be at peace. No weariness or wondering how the worlds problems would be solved in fact the only problem taking my attention was being solved with each "musical" keystroke. There was a sense of oneness with the code. An interrelatedness. As when one transitions from learning to swim to forgetting one is under the water. There is a time when one fears the water and struggles against it. For me even floating required serious sculling to keep from drowning, because of my supposed body type, but now floating can be sustained without sculling at all. The shift was from struggle to rest from fear to peace. Coding has become similar, it represents rest. When problems arrise I've learned to not fear or stress because of them the tension blocks ones mind just like tensing causes one to sink. Instead it becomes a time to let the mind expand. An opportunity to solve problems not yet solved or to explore the neaunces and obscurites of the code base.
